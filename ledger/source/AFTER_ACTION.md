# AFTER_ACTION.md — Reality Feedback

## Purpose

Capture real-world outcomes and feed them back into the system.

## Ownership

- OWNS: outcome logging, feedback loops, reality validation
- DOES NOT OWN: implementation, governance, escalation

---

## When to log

After:
- launching something
- executing a plan
- making a decision with measurable outcome
- completing a workflow that matters

---

## Format

Use the format that best fits the situation.

### Quantitative (when measurable)

- Action: what was done
- Expected: what was expected
- Result: what actually happened
- Gap: difference between expected and result
- Insight: what we learned
- Adjustment: what changes next time

---

### Qualitative (when not measurable)

- Action: what was done
- Outcome: what happened
- Learning: what we learned
- Adjustment: what changes next time

---

## Example

```
Action: launched landing page with X copy
Expected: 10% conversion
Result: 2% conversion
Gap: weak headline, unclear offer
Insight: value proposition not clear
Adjustment: simplify offer + stronger hook
```

---

## Logging Rule

Log only when:

- outcome is meaningful
- something was learned
- future behavior could change

Do NOT log:
- trivial actions
- obvious outcomes
- noise without insight

---

## Extraction Rule

Repeated patterns in AFTER_ACTION must be:

→ distilled into MEMORY (lessons)
→ or DECISIONS (rules)
→ or trigger SCOUT (improvement search)

Logs are temporary. Insights are permanent.

---

## System Connections

AFTER_ACTION:
- → updates MEMORY (lessons)
- → updates DECISIONS (patterns)
- → informs SCOUT (what to improve)
- → improves EXECUTOR (future actions)

---

## Principle

Without feedback, the system guesses better over time.
With feedback, the system knows better over time.

---

> 🧠 Final line
> You don't want more logs. You want logs that change how you act next time.
